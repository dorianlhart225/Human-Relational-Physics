# Research Ethics and Boundaries

This document outlines the ethical framework that guides all work in **Human Relational Physics (HRP)**. It explains how the project approaches sensitive topics of attachment, trauma bonding, emotional imprinting, and dangerous relational patterns while strictly adhering to “Do No Harm” principles.

These boundaries are foundational to the integrity and purpose of the entire framework.

## Guiding Principles
- **Do No Harm**: Refuse to retraumatize myself or others for the sake of data or insight.
- **Do No Avoidance**: Refuse to sidestep difficult questions when deeper understanding could prevent future harm.
- Protect dignity at every stage of inquiry and avoid forcing anyone (including myself) back into their worst wounds.
- Clearly distinguish between **survivors** (those affected by harmful relational patterns) and **perpetrators** (those who do the affecting), and study them using different methods and safeguards.
- Rely primarily on personal archives, survivor accounts, expert literature, anonymized data, and existing research. **No direct engagement with dangerous individuals** is required or pursued.
- Ground the approach in classical philosophy (Hippocrates’ “First, do no harm,” Socratic questioning, and Platonic inquiry) alongside modern trauma-informed ethics.

## Practical Boundaries
- Do not engage directly with perpetrators or high-risk individuals for research purposes.
- Work exclusively with safe, ethical sources: personal journals, poetry, anonymized lived experience, trusted confidants, and established research.
- When exploring ways to apply insights to healthier dynamics, use broad, universal framing rather than personal or triggering hypotheticals.
- Prioritize safety, dignity, and well-being over rapid data extraction or sensationalism.

## Purpose of This Ethical Framework
The goal of HRP is not blame, exposure, or sensationalism, but actionable understanding that supports:
- Prevention of harmful relational patterns
- Better risk assessment in attachment-related escalation (e.g., stalking, domestic violence)
- Improved support for survivors of trauma bonding and emotional imprinting
- Responsible development and application of core constructs such as the Irreversible Intimacy Principle, Recursive Imprint Cycle, and Orbital Attachment Theory

This framework ensures HRP remains truth-seeking, survivor-centered, and never coercive or exploitative.

## Connection to the Broader Project
These ethical boundaries are not optional constraints — they are what allow the framework to be developed responsibly and what will make its contributions meaningful and trustworthy in academic, clinical, and public contexts.
