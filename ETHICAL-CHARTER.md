## HRP Ethical Statement

Human Relational Physics (HRP) is developed with a strong commitment to ethical stewardship and the principle of **“Do No Harm.”**

The framework aims to help survivors and clinicians better understand persistent relational bonds, trauma imprinting, and post-intimacy dynamics. It is **not** intended to provide tools for manipulation, coercion, or control.

All public materials are heavily anonymized. Mechanistic details that could be misused are kept private or restricted to trusted professional contexts (therapists, researchers, or clinicians).

HRP prioritizes:
- Empowering the vulnerable
- Protecting participant privacy and safety
- Promoting healing and understanding rather than replication or exploitation
- Maintaining transparency about the framework’s dual-use potential

Any use of HRP concepts that seeks to harm, deceive, or restrict another person’s autonomy is explicitly contrary to the spirit and intent of this work.
