# Human Relational Physics (HRP)

An interdisciplinary framework for understanding adult attachment, emotional imprinting, and long-term relational dynamics.

## Overview
Human Relational Physics (HRP) models deep intimate bonds using physics-like concepts: irreversible phase changes, recursive cycles, orbital persistence, and divergent response patterns.

The framework integrates psychology, neuroscience, psychophysiology, and metaphorical physics to explain why certain relationships leave permanent marks and how those imprints continue to influence behavior across years — even decades — of separation.

## Universal Naming Convention (UNC)

Every file in this repository follows a consistent naming structure:  
**[AreaCode]-[TypeCode]-[StateCode]--[slug].md**

### Why this convention exists
- **Instant orientation**: You can tell at a glance *where* the note belongs, *what kind* of content it is, and *how mature* it is.
- **Easy navigation & search**: GitHub search, Obsidian, or any Markdown tool can quickly filter by prefix (e.g., all `SEL-*` or all `*-RS-*` files).
- **Lifecycle tracking**: Ideas and research evolve naturally from rough drafts to finished work.
- **Clarity for collaborators and future self**: No guessing required.

### Area Codes (Where this content lives)
- **SEL** — Self (cognition, identity, mental models)  
- **RES** — Research / technical  
- **SYS** — Systems / operations / meta  
*(Other areas such as FAI, MAR, CHI, etc., may appear as the framework expands)*

*These are navigational markers only — they carry no moral or priority weighting.*

### Type Codes (What kind of content it is)
- **ID** — Idea / musing (exploratory thoughts and theories in formation)  
- **AR** — Article / essay (developed writing)  
- **RS** — Research (formal notes, data, citations, technical depth)  
- **WF** — Workflow / process / method  
- **PF** — Profile (about a person or concept)  
- **NT** — General notes  
- **IN** — Inbox / capture (raw items)

### State Codes (Current lifecycle stage)
- **DR** — Draft (early / incomplete)  
- **AIP** — Answers in progress (actively developing)  
- **PEN** — Pended / paused (on hold)  
- **FN** — Finished (complete but not necessarily published)  
- **LIV** — Live / published  
- **ARC** — Archived / retired

**Usage tip**: Most early-stage theoretical work uses **ID** or **RS** with **DR** or **AIP**. Reserve **RES** area for rigorous, citation-heavy material.

**Example**: `SEL-RS-DR--hrp-ethical-charter-do-no-harm.md`

## Purpose & Ethics
HRP prioritizes ethical stewardship and operates under a strict “Do No Harm” charter. It is intended to help survivors understand and heal from persistent bonds — never to provide tools for manipulation or control.

All public materials are heavily anonymized. Raw data remains private.  
See [ETHICAL-CHARTER.md](ETHICAL-CHARTER.md) for full principles.
