# Human Relational Physics (HRP)

**An interdisciplinary framework for understanding adult attachment, emotional imprinting, and long-term relational dynamics.**

## Overview

Human Relational Physics (HRP) models deep intimate bonds using physics-like concepts: irreversible phase changes, recursive cycles, orbital persistence, and divergent response patterns.

The framework integrates psychology, neuroscience, psychophysiology, and metaphorical physics to explain why certain relationships leave permanent marks and how those imprints continue to influence behavior across years - even decades - of separation.

## Core Constructs

- **IIP – Irreversible Intimacy Principle**  
  Once intimacy crosses a critical threshold, individuals undergo a permanent shift in their baseline expectations of connection.

- **RIC – Recursive Imprint Cycle**  
  Post-IIP, attachment follows a predictable, self-reinforcing cycle with observable stages.

- **OAT – Orbital Attachment Theory**  
  Relational bonds behave like orbital systems: temporary distance is possible, but return is common due to persistent emotional gravity.

- **DRS – Divergence Response Spectrum**  
  After the same imprinting event, individuals diverge into distinct response classes (integration, destabilization, control, rejection/externalization).

- **LSI – Law of Sensory Imprint**  
  High-intensity sensory and emotional engagement creates a strong, lifelong reference pattern.

- **SIP – Somatic Interference Principle** (candidate)  
  When trauma becomes associated with an imprinted bond, the nervous system may inhibit embodied engagement even when cognitive desire persists.

## Purpose & Ethics

HRP prioritizes **ethical stewardship** and operates under a strict “Do No Harm” charter. It is intended to help survivors understand and heal from persistent bonds — never to provide tools for manipulation or control.

All public materials are heavily anonymized. Raw data remains private.
